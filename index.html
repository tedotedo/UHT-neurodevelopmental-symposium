Programme direction

Built around the everyday decisions that shape care.

The 2026 symposium is planned around practical paediatric neurodevelopmental medicine — bridging current research, specialist expertise and day-to-day clinical excellence.

Core themes include cerebral palsy, autism spectrum disorder, genetics, epilepsy, neurocutaneous syndromes, movement disorders, mental health, palliative communication and transition of care.

01

Cerebral palsy & spasticity

Modern approaches to spasticity management and functional outcomes in children with neurodisability.

02

Autism, mental health & anxiety

Recognising hidden mental-health difficulties in children with neurodevelopmental disorders.

03

Genetics & neurocutaneous disorders

Applying genomic insight to developmental disorders and complex clinical presentations.

04

Epilepsy & movement disorders

Clinical reasoning for seizure mimics, movement disorders and neurology-adjacent presentations.

05

Palliative communication

Words that heal: compassionate communication with children and families facing neurodisability.

06

Transition of care

Supporting continuity as children and young people move through services and into adult pathways.

Who should attend

For senior paediatric and allied professionals.

  • Consultant paediatricians
  • Specialist trainees
  • Advanced nurse practitioners
  • Paediatric therapists
  • Senior nursing staff
